About

A Working Woman’s Fight is a project created by UCLA students and faculty in completion of Digital Humanities 199, a course in which students receive a humanities-based dataset and collaborate to present it in the form of a digital project.

At the beginning of spring quarter of 2018, Professor Higbie and Professor Luce provided our group of six undergraduate students with a wealth of data about LA garment workers, hotel workers and janitors. While parsing through images, video footage, demographic and geographic data, we were stricken by female workers’ strength and resilience as they fought not only for better wages and working conditions, but for better lives for themselves and their families. We decided to use our unique set of skills to tell working women’s stories in compelling, accessible ways.

Wendy Dorantes created maps to make visible the history of the LA garment industry, which has long operated under the radar yet under many of our noses. Maggie Shi created an infographic to provide information about the workers themselves, from their countries of origin to the number of hours they work each day to the clothing brands they produce for. Holly Raymond stitched together video clips capturing individual women’s struggles - living in poverty due to low wages, experiencing health issues due to difficult working conditions, and facing disrespectful treatment from employers - to tell a story that is universal to many working women. Hannah Pham and Kim Truong built and designed this website to house our maps, infographics and videos, and to present working women’s stories in dynamic ways. Emily Parsons wrote content (including this very description) to clarify the project’s message and to tie each component into a cohesive narrative.

We hope that our project will help make visible the women who sew, clean and serve others in Los Angeles, and that their stories will resonate with you.
