1920s to 1940s Manufacturers

These maps are made through the use of inventory data collected from the Los Angeles Labor center. These specific points mark where manufacturers set up shop from the 1920s to the 1940s. Factories centered around Los Angeles and expanded throughout the city and the valley as the century continued. Workers moved around these areas to be able to work. These first factories established the foundation for where these shops continue to operate today, unseen to many but not to these workers.

The map is best used by clicking through the different layers. Each layer is a different data set that locates manufacturing factories. The map overlay shows the focus of all these layers throughout the county.

2000s Intake Industries Forms

This map shows where these workers are coming from and how they have moved in order to be able to work in these factories. Many of these workers are women who have moved from different countries to make less than minimum wage to help support their families.

About the Project

These maps are a visual representation on how the industry has grown over time in Los Angeles. The manufacturing industry continues to thrive in Los Angeles, however it is not visible to many. The maps provide a tangible view into an industry that operates under the radar and affects the surrounding communities.
